Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hard drive (HDD)?

−

A hard disk drive (HDD) is a data storage device that uses magnetic storage to store and retrieve digital information. HDDs are widely used in computers, NAS systems, surveillance setups, and enterprise data centers due to their high capacity and cost-effectiveness compared to solid-state drives.

What's the difference between internal and external HDDs?

+

Internal HDDs are installed inside your computer or NAS enclosure and connect via SATA or SAS interfaces for maximum performance. External HDDs come in their own enclosure with USB connectivity, making them portable and easy to use with any computer without opening the case.

How much HDD storage do I need?

+

The right amount of storage depends on your use case. For basic desktop use, 1-2TB is sufficient. Gamers typically need 4-6TB, while NAS users often start at 8TB and scale up. Enterprise and surveillance applications may require 18TB or more per drive.

HDD vs SSD: which should I choose?

+

SSDs are faster and more durable but cost more per gigabyte. HDDs offer significantly more storage capacity at lower prices, making them ideal for bulk storage, NAS systems, backups, and surveillance. Many users combine both: an SSD for the operating system and frequently used files, and an HDD for mass storage.

Are Western Digital HDDs compatible with Mac and Windows?

+

Yes. Western Digital internal HDDs can be formatted to work with both Mac (APFS, HFS+) and Windows (NTFS) systems. External HDDs may come pre-formatted for one system but can be reformatted for compatibility. Some models also support exFAT for cross-platform use out of the box.

How long do hard drives last?

+

The average lifespan of an HDD is 3 to 5 years, though many drives last significantly longer with proper care. Enterprise-grade drives like WD Gold and Ultrastar are built for higher workloads and typically come with longer warranty periods. Regular backups are always recommended regardless of drive reliability.
